Magazine homepages often need a middle layer between the lead story and the general feed. That is where a spotlight column works well.

It can feature satire, politics, AI releases, project writeups, or any category you want readers to notice first. In Jekyll, the content can come from the same post collection while the homepage decides how to present it.

## Why it helps

- It gives one section a stronger identity.
- It breaks up a long list of article cards.
- It feels more like a curated front page than a plain blog feed.
